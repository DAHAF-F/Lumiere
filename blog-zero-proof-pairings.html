Zero-Proof, Full Flavor: Alcohol-Free Pairings at LUMIÈRE

By Elena Marchetti · 18 April 2026 · 5 min read

← Back to all blogs

Not every guest at the table drinks, and that should never mean a smaller experience. Here's how we build a zero-proof pairing that does the same job as a wine list — course by course, with real acidity, tannin and weight.

Why zero-proof deserves the same care as wine

A pairing exists to do one job: make the next bite taste better than it would alone. Alcohol isn't what does that — acidity, bitterness, tannin, temperature and aroma are. Once we treated the zero-proof list as its own discipline rather than a substitute, it stopped being an afterthought and became one of the most requested pairings on the menu.

What's actually in the glass

  • Shrubs and verjus: unripe grape and fruit acids that stand in for a wine's cut.
  • Tea and koji ferments: built in-house for tannin and savoury depth.
  • Dealcoholized wine bases: used sparingly, layered with fresh aromatics.
  • Cordials and infusions: Cypriot citrus, mountain herbs, and roasted stone fruit.

How we pair, course by course

The scallop course gets a yuzu-kosho and green tea cordial for lift. The dry-aged duck is matched with a smoked cherry and hibiscus shrub built to mirror a light red's tannin. By dessert, a roasted honey and chamomile infusion does what a dessert wine would — without a drop of alcohol in the glass.

A good zero-proof pairing isn't a mocktail on the side. It's a drink built to do a wine's job, at the same pace as the menu.

Booking the zero-proof pairing

It's available on every tasting menu at LUMIÈRE, alongside our natural wine pairing — just mention it when you book. You can see both options on the current menu.
