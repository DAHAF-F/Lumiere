The Mediterranean Catch of the Day: A Morning at the Limassol Fish Market

By Elena Marchetti · 8 December 2025 · 5 min read

← Back to all blogs

Our seafood course is decided before 7am, on a quay in Limassol, by a supplier we've worked with since we opened — not by a catalogue and not by us.

Small boats, not trawlers

Everything we serve comes off single-day fishing boats working the coast around Limassol, not industrial trawlers. It's a smaller, slower supply chain, and it means the fish on the pass that night was in the sea that morning.

What actually decides the fish course

We don't call ahead with an order. We show up and buy what the boats brought in — sea bream one week, red mullet the next, cuttlefish or the occasional prized turbot after that. The menu bends to the catch, not the other way round, which is also covered in our piece on seasonal Cypriot ingredients.

Nothing wasted

We buy the whole fish, not fillets. Bones and heads go straight into the stocks and sauces that tie a seafood course together — it's a big part of why the flavour tastes specific to this coast rather than generic "fish course."

The best fish course we ever serve is the one we didn't plan for a week ago.

What's on the boat this week

It changes constantly, which is the point. Check the current menu for what made it onto the pass this week, or book a table and let us surprise you.
